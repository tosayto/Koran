

Chapter 70

Lofty Stations
al-Maʿārij ( المعارج )

44 verses • revealed at Meccan


»The surah that proclaims God to be the Lord of the heavenly ascents, Lofty Stations, the points of rising from which the angels ascend to God. It takes is named after the phrase “of lofty stations” (dhi al-maʿārij) in verse 3. The surah describes the Day of Judgement (verse 8 ff.). One of the opponents of the Prophet challenged him to hasten the punishment they had been threatened with (verse 1), so the foolishness of the disbelievers in denying the Resurrection (verse 6) is exposed (verse 36 ff.). The people who will be granted the Garden are described (verse 22 ff.).« 

The surah is also known as: Elevations, Staircases Upward, The Ascending Steps, The Heavenly Ascents, The Ladders, The Stairways, The Steps, The Ways of Ascent.

To Him the angels and the Spirit mount up, in a day whereof the measure is fifty thousand years.


Muhammad Asad


all the angels and all the inspiration [ever granted to man] ascend unto Him1 [daily,] in a day the length whereof is [like] fifty thousand years.2


1.For my rendering of ruh as "inspiration", see surah 16, note 2. The "ascent" of the angels and of all inspiration may be understood in the same sense as the frequently-occurring phrase "all things go back to God [as their source]" (Razi).
2.The very concept of "time" is meaningless in relation to God, who is timeless and infinite: cf. note 63 on the last sentence of 22:47 - "in thy Sustainer's sight a day is like a thousand years of your reckoning": in other words, a day, or an aeon, or a thousand years, or fifty thousand years are alike to Him, having an apparent reality only within the created world and none with the Creator. And since in the hereafter time will cease to have a meaning for man as well, it is irrelevant to ask as to "when" the evildoers will be chastised and the righteous given their due.

۞ إِنَّ الإِنسانَ خُلِقَ هَلوعًا



A. J. Arberry

Muhammad Asad


VERILY, man is born with a restless disposition.1


1.Lit., "man has been created restless (halu'an)" - that is, endowed with an inner restlessness which may equally well drive him to fruitful achievement or to chronic discontent and frustration. In other words, it is the manner in which man utilizes this God - will endowment that determines whether it shall have a positive or a negative character. The subsequent two verses (20 and 21) allude to the latter, while verses 22-25 show that only true spiritual and moral consciousness can mould that inborn restlessness into a positive force, and thus bring about inner stability and abiding contentment.

A. J. Arberry


No! I swear by the Lord of the Easts and Wests, surely We are able


Muhammad Asad


But nay! I call to witness [Our being] the Sustainer of all the points of sunrise and sunset:1 verily, well able are We


1.I.e., of all the variation, throughout the solar year, of the points at which the sun "rises" and "sets": thus stressing the fact that He is the Ultimate Cause of all orbital movement in the universe and, hence, its Creator (cf. 37:5 and 55:17).


J. M. Rodwell


It needs not that I swear by the Lord of the East and of the West that we have power.


E. H. Palmer


And I need not swear by the Lord of the easts and the wests; verily, we are able


George Sale


I swear by the Lord of the east and of the west, that We are able to destroy them,




41



عَلىٰ أَن نُبَدِّلَ خَيرًا مِنهُم وَما نَحنُ بِمَسبوقينَ



Fred Leemhuis


om hen door iets beters te vervangen en niemand kan Ons voor zijn.


Salomon Keyzer


(Hen te verdelgen en) een beter volk voor hen in de plaats te stellen; en niemand kan het verhinderen, indien het ons behaagt dit te doen.


Sofjan Siregar


Om hen te vervangen (door anderen die) beter dan hen zijn en Wij zijn niet zwak.


Edip Yüksel


Onları, kendilerinden daha iyilerle değiştirmeye... Bizi kimse yenemez


Yasar Nuri Ozturk


Onları kendilerinden daha üstün olanlarla değiştirmeye... Ve biz önüne geçilebilecekler değiliz.






عَلىٰ أَن نُبَدِّلَ خَيرًا مِنهُم وَما نَحنُ بِمَسبوقينَ



A. J. Arberry


to substitute a better than they; We shall not be outstripped.


Muhammad Asad


to replace them with [people] better than they are; for there is nothing to prevent Us [from doing what We will].1


1.The implication is that it is not His will to replace "those who are bent on denying the truth", in this world, by believers, inasmuch as such a "replacement" would not be in accord with His design of multiform human existence, in which faith is always challenged and tested by unbelief, and vice versa.


J. M. Rodwell


To replace them with better than themselves: neither are we to be hindered.


E. H. Palmer


to change them for others better, nor are we prevented!


George Sale


and to substitute better than them in their room; neither are We to be prevented, if We shall please so to do.




42



فَذَرهُم يَخوضوا وَيَلعَبوا حَتّىٰ يُلاقوا يَومَهُمُ الَّذي يوعَدونَ



Fred Leemhuis


Laat hen maar kletsen en schertsen totdat zij hun dag tegenkomen die hun wordt aangezegd,


Salomon Keyzer


Daarom, laat hen in ijdele gesprekken waden, en in vermaken genot zoeken, tot zij den dag ontmoeten, waarmede zij bedreigd zijn.


Sofjan Siregar


Laat hen maar opgaan (in hun ijdele bezigheden) en laat hen spelen tot zij de Dag van hen, die hen aangezegd was, ontmoeten.


Edip Yüksel


Bırak onları, kendilerine söz verilen gün ile karşı karşıya gelinceye kadar dalsınlar, oynasınlar.


Yasar Nuri Ozturk


Bırak onları! Dalsınlar, oynasınlar kendileri için belirlenen günlerine ulaşıncaya kadar.






فَذَرهُم يَخوضوا وَيَلعَبوا حَتّىٰ يُلاقوا يَومَهُمُ الَّذي يوعَدونَ



A. J. Arberry


Then leave them alone to plunge and play until they encounter that day of theirs which they are promised,


Muhammad Asad


Hence, leave them to indulge in idle talk and play [with words]1 until they face that [Judgment] Day of theirs which they have been promised


1.I.e., their philosophizing about a supposedly "uncreated" world and a hypothetical "self-generation" of life, as well as their blatant "denial", unsupported by any factual evidence, of a life after death or even of the existence of God.


J. M. Rodwell


Wherefore let them flounder on and disport them, till they come face to face with their threatened day,


E. H. Palmer


So leave them to plunge in discussion, and to play until they meet that day of theirs which they are threatened with,


George Sale


Wherefore suffer them to wade in vain disputes, and to amuse themselves with sport: Until they meet their day with which they have been threatened;




43



يَومَ يَخرُجونَ مِنَ الأَجداثِ سِراعًا كَأَنَّهُم إِلىٰ نُصُبٍ يوفِضونَ



Fred Leemhuis


de dag waarop zij snel uit de graven tevoorschijn komen alsof zij zich naar hun afgod haasten,


Salomon Keyzer


Den dag waarop zij haastig uit hunne graven zullen voortkomen, als scharen, die zich naar hunne vanen spoeden.


Sofjan Siregar


Op de Dag waarop zij zich haastig uit hun graven spoeden, alsof zij zich naar afgodsbeelden spoeden.


Edip Yüksel


O gün mezarlarından hızlı hızlı çıkarlar; kurban taşına sürülüyorlarmış gibi...


Yasar Nuri Ozturk


O gün, kabirlerden fırlayarak çıkarlar. Dikilmiş putlara doğru akın akın gider gibidirler.






يَومَ يَخرُجونَ مِنَ الأَجداثِ سِراعًا كَأَنَّهُم إِلىٰ نُصُبٍ يوفِضونَ



A. J. Arberry


the day they shall come forth from the tombs hastily, as if they were hurrying unto a waymark,


Muhammad Asad


the Day when they shall come forth in haste from their graves, as if racing towards a goal-post,


J. M. Rodwell


The day on which they shall flock up out of their graves in haste like men who rally to a standard: -


E. H. Palmer


the day when they shall come forth in haste from the graves, as though they flock to a standard!


George Sale


the day whereon they shall come forth hastily from their graves, as though they were troops hastening to their standard:

خاشِعَةً أَبصارُهُم تَرهَقُهُم ذِلَّةٌ ۚ ذٰلِكَ اليَومُ الَّذي كانوا يوعَدونَ



Fred Leemhuis


terwijl zij met hun deemoedige blikken met vernedering overdekt worden. Dat is de dag die hun was aangezegd.


Salomon Keyzer


Hunne blikken zullen nedergeslagen zijn, en schande zal hen volgen. Dit is de dag, waarmede zij bedreigd zijn geworden.


Sofjan Siregar


Hun blikken angstig teneergeslagen, overladen met vernedering. Dit is de Dag die jullie werd aangezegd.


Edip Yüksel


Gözleri dönmüş, kendilerini utanç sarmış olarak. İşte bu, onlara söz verilmiş olan gündür.


Yasar Nuri Ozturk


Gözleri yere eğik; bir zillet kuşatmıştır onları. İşte bu gündür onlara vaat edilmiş olan.













خاشِعَةً أَبصارُهُم تَرهَقُهُم ذِلَّةٌ ۚ ذٰلِكَ اليَومُ الَّذي كانوا يوعَدونَ



A. J. Arberry


humbled their eyes, overspreading them abasement. That is the day which they were promised.


Muhammad Asad


with downcast eyes, with ignominy overwhelming them: that Day which they were promised again and again....1


1.The concept of "again and again" - i.e., by a succession, through the ages, of prophetic revelations - is implied in the auxiliary verb kanu, which usually connotes repetition and/or duration.


J. M. Rodwell


Their eyes downcast; disgrace shall cover them. Such their threatened day. 


E. H. Palmer


with their looks abashed; meanness shall cover them! That is the day which they were promised! 


George Sale


Their looks shall be downcast; ignominy shall attend them. This is the day with which they have been threatened.
